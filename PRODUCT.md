# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Kutoarjo Social Club serves two equally important groups. Customers use the public site on mobile or desktop to understand the venue, compare padel and billiard options, check practical details, book a billiard table, and continue payment confirmation through WhatsApp. Staff and admins use the authenticated surface during daily operations to manage bookings, schedules, walk-ins, CMS content, and manual payment follow-up.

## Product Purpose

The product exists to make Kutoarjo Social Club easier to discover, book, and operate. Public visitors should quickly trust the venue, find the right activity, and secure a billiard slot without confusion. Internal users should be able to keep schedules accurate, handle walk-ins, update content, and resolve booking status without fighting the interface.

Success means fewer booking questions, fewer schedule conflicts, faster staff handling, clearer payment follow-up, and a public experience that feels as organized as the club wants to be perceived.

## Positioning

A local sports club website that connects public discovery, billiard booking, and staff operations in one clear workflow.

## Brand Personality

Sporty, tidy, and welcoming. The interface should feel active without becoming loud, operationally precise without becoming cold, and local without feeling informal or unfinished.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS dashboards, overly decorative sports graphics, nightclub-like darkness, vague luxury styling, tiny low-contrast text, busy icon-card grids, and gimmicky motion that slows users who just want to book or manage a slot.

## Design Principles

- Keep the path to booking obvious from every public surface.
- Treat schedule accuracy as the core trust signal.
- Make staff actions fast, legible, and hard to misread.
- Let the club feel active through rhythm, contrast, and copy, not visual noise.
- Preserve one visual language across public pages and admin tools.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for contrast, focus states, form labels, keyboard navigation, and responsive layout. Motion should respect reduced-motion preferences. Public booking must remain readable and usable on mobile screens and ordinary local network conditions.
